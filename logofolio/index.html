Logofolio

Custom, strategy-driven logos that don’t depend on color, scale and composition. While trends come and go, these logos keep the essence of the brand going.

5-Eleven Hoops

5-Eleven Hoops is a basketball initiative for children with autism spectrum disorders. It got a sporty logo with an infinite symbol depicting both autism and hoops.

Those little dots are aimed at 5 and 11 o’clock. This small feature makes it possible to register the infinite symbol separately.

CityUpdate

CityUpdate is a social initiative and an urbanists’ movement. It got a contemporary Modernism-inspired logo that is just midway “C” and “U”, and looks like a bird view on a building.

Evergreen Mortgage

Evergreen Mortgage is an equity based mortgage lender. They got a pine cone mark to symbolize protected, risk-free growth.

Franco

Franco is a construction company specializing on concrete buildings. It got a logo that is assembled from parts, just like the buildings.

Greenverd

Greenverd is an eco-friendly online store. Their vision is “evolution of zero waste”. So the logo has got an arrow formed by reduction of the letter “G”.

Inspiration Family

Inspiration Family is a cancer foundation. It got a cozy and joyful logo that depicts different stages of cell’s growth.

There are also letters “I” and “F” in the mark. Have you spotted them already?

Living Mirror

Living Mirror is a playback theater, an improv theater “reflecting” the audience’s stories. Thus, the letter “O” is shaped like a mirror.

Macramé

Macramé is a dating service. So the logo looks both like macramé, and like people holding hands.

Mitipi

Mitipi is a smart insurance company. Its logo resembles the shape of the key product, and also reminds of the main feature — projecting light.

NOTS

Norsk Organisasjon For Terrengsykling is an association of mountain bikers. Thus, the letter “O” is a bicycle wheel with a spoke nipple.

It also got NASA-like font. And no, those are not worms, those are roads :)

Skole

Skole is a Ukrainian touristic town. Following the meaning of the name (which is similar to “split, cleaved”), the top parts of the logo are chopped with axe.
